I really enjoy javascript, thanks to Javascript: The Good Parts, by Douglas Crockford. I spend most of my time coding in C, thanks to the sheer volume of schoolwork I have that requires it, and I do love C. But I also love first-class functions, and javascript's ridiculous typing system. Recently I found myself writing some Java where my function did not just run over 80 characters, it ran over several multiples of 80! After trauma like that, being able to declare a function like: var foo = function(bar, baz) {} is liberating. I'm interested in other (very-)high level languages like lisp/scheme and parts of python.
